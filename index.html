Sobolev-Ozok Lattice (SOL)

The Sobolev-Ozok Lattice (SOL) is a Planck-scale lattice framework in which spacetime curvature, quantum dynamics, and physical constants emerge from coherence and resolution interactions between discrete cells.

Core Idea

  • Spacetime is modeled as a discrete lattice of Planck-scale cells.
  • Coherence gradients between cells generate an effective geometric structure (metric).
  • Quantum behavior arises from resolution dynamics rather than postulates.

Scope

  • Emergent metric and recovery of General Relativity at macroscopic scales.
  • Derivation of physical constants from lattice coherence structure.
  • Applications to solar system tests, galaxy rotation, and black hole phenomena.

Predictions & Falsifiability

Higher-order coherence corrections (k ≥ 2) introduce small, scale-dependent deviations from standard General Relativity. These deviations are testable in planetary precession, galaxy rotation curves, and black hole shadow profiles.
